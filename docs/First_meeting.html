Kick-off meeting - Friday 31st March 2023

The misappropriation of genetics


The kick-off meeting focused on the misappropriation of genetics and was based on a talk by Jedidiah Carlson as well as his Twitter thread (@JedMSP). We highly recommend having a look at Jedidiah Carlson’s Twitter thread, as he discusses important issues - such as the existence of extremist ‘journal clubs’, the ‘meme-ification’ of figures (particularly PCS plots) and the interest in research on the genetics of cognitive/behavioral traits (particularly IQ).
In addition, we briefly considered the 2002 paper Genetic Structure of Human Populations by Noah Rosenberg et al., which was the most cited paper in Science in its year of publication, and which continues to be cited in scientific and philosophical debates until the present day. Our main aim, however, was not to discuss its scientific findings, but to discuss why this paper has repeatedly been cited as evidence for a biological basis to race by looking at the paper Are Clusters Races? A Discussion of the Rhetorical Appropriation of Rosenberg et al.’s ‘Genetic Structure of Human Populations’ by Melissa Wills, which was published fifteen years later.
The first meeting ended with a discussion about how we as researchers can decrease the probability of our research being misappropriated.
