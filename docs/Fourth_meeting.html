Fourth meeting - Friday 18th August 2023

Discussion - Embryonic Screening (part 1)


The fourth ADWG meeting was used to discuss the topic from the last meeting, namely the implementation and use of embryonic screening and the concerns that arise in this connection.

The meeting started with a brief recap of the topic presented at the previous meeting. Afterwards, we turned to some of the bioethical thought experiments presented in Chapter 40, Red Lines, of the book The code breaker: Jennifer Doudna, gene editing, and the future of the human race written by Walter Isaacson. Note that all users of Det Kgl. Bibliotek are able to read this book online (for free) at kb.dk.
Even though this book focuses on the concerns that arise in connection to gene editing with CRISPR, the thought experiments are also applicable to genetic screening.
In this meeting, we focused on the thought experiments about monogenic diseases (Huntington’s disease and Sickle-cell anemia), as well as deafness (and the associated discussion about disadvantages vs. disabilities). Regarding the latter, we considered a real life example from 2002 of a deaf lesbian couple wanting a deaf baby (read more about this case here), and discussed the ethical concerns that were raised by society. A paper discussing some ethical issues can be found here.


We highly recommend reading the review Concerns about the use of polygenic embryo screening for psychiatric and cognitive traits, published by Todd Lencz et al. in Lancet Psychiatry in 2022, as well as Chapter 40, Red Lines, of the book The code breaker: Jennifer Doudna, gene editing, and the future of the human race written by Walter Isaacson. Again we want to highlight that all users of Det Kgl. Bibliotek are able to read this book online (for free) at kb.dk.
