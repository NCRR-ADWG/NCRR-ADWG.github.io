9th meeting - Thursday 21st of March 2024

The Danish Brains - a bioethical discussion


In this anti-discrimination working group meeting we learned about the Danish Brains from our guest speaker Thomas Erslev.
Thomas Erslev is a medical historian who has been working with medical collection practices and the ethical controversies attendant on such practices for several years. Thomas wrote his PhD-thesis about The Danish Brains, a collection of 9,479 brains taken from psychiatric patients who were autopsied for diagnostic and research purposes at Danish mental hospitals between 1945 and 1982. You can learn more about the Danish Brains from this BBC clip or this CNN documentary.
After learning about the background of the Danish Brains, we discussed some of the bioethical dilemmas surrounding biobanks and register data in the context of psychiatry.
